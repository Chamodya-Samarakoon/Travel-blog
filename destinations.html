My Travel Stories

Sigiriya, Sri Lanka

Sigiriya, often called the “Lion Rock,” is an ancient rock fortress and palace built in the 5th century. Rising nearly 200 meters above the surrounding plains, it offers breathtaking views of the lush jungle below. The site is famous for its giant lion-shaped gateway, stunning frescoes, mirror wall, and beautifully designed water gardens. Today, Sigiriya stands as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of Sri Lanka’s rich history.

Highlights & Activities

  • Climb to the top of Sigiriya Rock Fortress
  • Explore the ancient frescoes
  • Visit the surrounding water gardens

Nuwara Eliya, Sri Lanka

Nuwara Eliya, fondly known as “Little England,” is a charming hill station nestled in Sri Lanka’s central highlands. Surrounded by rolling tea plantations, misty mountains, and colonial-era architecture, the city offers a refreshing cool climate. Visitors can enjoy boating on Gregory Lake, strolling through Hakgala Botanical Garden, and touring local tea factories. Its unique blend of nature, history, and culture makes it a favorite escape for both locals and travelers.

Highlights & Activities

  • Visit tea plantations and factories
  • Boat ride on Gregory Lake
  • Explore Hakgala Botanical Garden

Paris, France

Paris, the world-famous “City of Light,” is renowned for its timeless elegance, romance, and culture. From iconic landmarks like the Eiffel Tower and the Louvre to charming cafés and cobblestone streets, the city offers endless experiences. Art lovers can marvel at masterpieces, fashion enthusiasts can shop in luxury boutiques, and foodies can indulge in French cuisine. Paris is a place where history and modernity blend seamlessly, making it one of the most visited cities in the world.

Highlights & Activities

  • Visit the Eiffel Tower
  • Explore the Louvre Museum
  • Stroll along the Seine River

Bali, Indonesia

Bali, often called the “Island of the Gods,” is a tropical paradise that blends natural beauty with deep cultural traditions. With golden beaches, lush rice terraces, and ancient temples, it offers something for every kind of traveler. Adventure seekers can surf, dive, or trek volcanic mountains, while others may prefer relaxing in beach resorts or experiencing Balinese yoga and spa retreats. The island’s vibrant festivals, warm hospitality, and breathtaking landscapes make it a dream destination.

Highlights & Activities

  • Relax on Kuta Beach
  • Discover Uluwatu Temple
  • Enjoy Balinese dance and cuisine
